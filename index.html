About AWBK

Guardians of Animal Welfare in Karnataka

The Animal Welfare Board of Karnataka (AWBK) is a statutory body constituted under the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act, 1960. It operates under the aegis of the Department of Animal Husbandry and Veterinary Sciences, Government of Karnataka.

Our mission is to advise the State Government on matters relating to animal welfare, enforce the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act, and promote humane treatment of all animals across Karnataka's 30 districts.

  • Enforcement of the PCA Act, 1960 across Karnataka
  • Animal Birth Control (ABC) programme oversight
  • Licensing of pet shops, gaushalas & animal shelters
  • School & community awareness programmes statewide
  • Emergency rescue coordination with local bodies

Our Journey

History of Animal Welfare in Karnataka

From a nascent movement to a robust statutory body — six decades of compassion.

1955

The Rukmini Devi Movement

Renowned Bharatanatyam dancer and animal rights champion Rukmini Devi Arundale begins advocacy at the national level, inspiring Karnataka's early animal welfare volunteers.

1960

PCA Act Enacted

The Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act, 1960 is passed by the Indian Parliament, providing the legal foundation for animal welfare bodies including Karnataka's chapter.

1962

AWBI Established — Karnataka Chapter Formed

The Animal Welfare Board of India is constituted under Rukmini Devi Arundale's chairpersonship. Karnataka forms its own state chapter under the Department of Animal Husbandry.

1975

First Animal Shelter Network

AWBK establishes the first network of government-recognised animal shelters across Bengaluru, Mysuru and Dharwad, housing and rehabilitating injured street animals.

1995

ABC Programme Launched

Karnataka becomes one of the first states to implement a systematic Animal Birth Control programme in collaboration with municipalities to humanely manage stray dog populations.

2008

Wildlife Corridor Protection Initiative

AWBK partners with the Forest Department to protect wildlife corridors across the Western Ghats, safeguarding elephants, leopards, and other fauna from human-wildlife conflict.

2020

Digital Transformation & Helpline 1962

Launch of dedicated cruelty helpline 1962 and the online complaint portal, enabling 24×7 reporting of animal abuse across Karnataka. Mobile veterinary units deployed in 12 districts.

2024

Expanding Coverage & New Legislation

AWBK now oversees 450+ registered shelters, 30 district offices, and advocates for stricter penalties under the proposed Prevention of Cruelty to Animals (Amendment) Bill.
